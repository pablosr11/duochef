# Growth Experiment: Landing Page Headline A/B Test

**Objective:** To determine which headline on the ChefAtHome landing page drives the highest conversion rate for waitlist sign-ups.

**Hypothesis:** A headline that more directly addresses the pain point of cooking intimidation will outperform a headline that focuses on the 'Duolingo for Cooking' analogy.

**Experiment Design:**

1.  **Control (Version A):** The current landing page headline: "Learn to Cook Like You Learn a Language"
2.  **Variant (Version B):** New headline: "Stop Ordering In, Start Cooking Confidently!"

**Key Metrics:**

*   Waitlist sign-up conversion rate (number of sign-ups / number of unique visitors).
*   Bounce rate.
*   Time on page.

**Target Audience:** New visitors to the ChefAtHome landing page.

**Duration:** 2 weeks, or until statistically significant results are achieved.

**Implementation:**

*   Utilize an A/B testing tool (e.g., Google Optimize, Optimizely) to split traffic 50/50 between Version A and Version B.
*   Ensure tracking is in place to accurately measure conversions and other key metrics.

**Success Criteria:** The headline variant that results in a statistically significant increase (e.g., p-value < 0.05) in waitlist sign-up conversion rate will be considered the winner and implemented permanently.
