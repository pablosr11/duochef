# ChefAtHome: User Feedback & Early Acquisition Plan

## Goal:
To gather valuable user feedback during the early stages of ChefAtHome and to implement effective strategies for early user acquisition, focusing on our target audience of complete beginners and busy adults.

## User Feedback Collection:

1.  **In-App Feedback Mechanism:**
    *   Implement a simple, non-intrusive way for users to submit feedback directly within the app. This could be a "Send Feedback" button in the settings or a prompt after completing a lesson.
    *   Focus on qualitative feedback: "What did you like?", "What was confusing?", "What would you like to learn next?"

2.  **Post-Lesson Surveys (Short & Targeted):**
    *   After a user completes a set of core lessons (e.g., Level 0 or Level 1), present a very short survey (2-3 questions) to gauge their confidence and understanding.
    *   Example questions: "How confident do you feel performing X task after this lesson? (Scale 1-5)", "Would you recommend this lesson to a friend? (Yes/No)"

3.  **Community Building (Future Phase):**
    *   As the user base grows, consider creating a dedicated community forum or Discord server where users can share experiences, ask questions, and provide feedback.

4.  **Direct Outreach:**
    *   For users who opt-in for communication, conduct occasional email surveys or short interviews to gather more in-depth insights.

## Early Acquisition Strategy:

1.  **Leverage Waitlist Momentum:**
    *   Continue to promote the waitlist with targeted social media ads and organic posts.
    *   Engage waitlist subscribers with updates and exclusive content leading up to launch.

2.  **Influencer/Micro-Influencer Outreach:**
    *   Identify food bloggers, home economics educators, or lifestyle influencers with audiences that align with our target demographic (beginners, busy adults).
    *   Offer early access or a partnership to review and promote ChefAtHome.

3.  **Content Marketing:**
    *   Create blog posts and social media content around beginner cooking tips, kitchen safety, and the benefits of learning practical skills – subtly integrating ChefAtHome as a solution.
    *   Guest post on relevant blogs or participate in online cooking communities.

4.  **App Store Optimization (ASO):**
    *   Ensure app store listings (title, description, keywords, screenshots) are optimized to attract our target audience.
    *   Highlight the unique "Duolingo for Cooking" approach and the benefits of skill-based learning.

5.  **Referral Program (Post-Launch):**
    *   Implement a simple referral program to incentivize existing users to invite friends.

## Key Metrics:

*   **Feedback:** Number of feedback submissions, survey completion rates, sentiment analysis of qualitative feedback.
*   **Acquisition:** Waitlist sign-ups, website traffic, app downloads, cost per acquisition (CPA), user retention rates for early adopters.
